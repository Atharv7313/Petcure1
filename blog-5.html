Understanding International Pet Relocation Requirements

Relocating Your Pet Overseas

Relocating your pet overseas will be a complex task without knowing what exactly to do before shipping your furry friend. However, the right knowledge and support from pet relocation services ensure this task is safe and stress-free. In this blog, we let you walk through the key considerations for international pet relocation and how AVC helps you in this process.

You Need to Understand the Pet Import Regulations

When it comes to pet relocation, every nation has its own pet import requirements. It is important to learn the unique requirement procedure of the destination you choose to ship your furry companion. Usually, the majority of countries around the globe seek requirements such as:

  • Vaccination records
  • Microchipping
  • Health certificates
  • Quarantine rules

AVC, with our vast network of pet relocation services, provides complete details regarding the specific country import rules to ease your plan.

You Need to Update Your Petâs Vaccination Status

It is essential to have rabies vaccination for international pet relocation. Some countries require:

  • Detailed vaccines
  • Rabies titer test results

Consult with our experienced staff in pet relocation services to ensure that your pet meets the specific requirements for your selected destination.

You Need to Create a Timeline for Pet Transport Preparations

For international pet relocation, you need to start planning early, as it may require 1 to 3 months of preparation time. However, for rabies-free nations such as:

  • Australia
  • New Zealand

The timeline may extend up to 3 months. Therefore, consider your destination and schedule accordingly.

You Need to Choose the Right Travel Crate

During the pet relocation process, your pet needs to be shipped in an airline-approved travel crate with the right size to ensure their comfort. Acclimate your furry companion to the crate in advance to minimize anxiety during travel.

Microchip: An Essential Identical Tool During Pet Relocation

Microchips are mostly needed for international pet relocation as they meet ISO standard 11784. While it is not compulsory, we recommend microchipping your companion for identification and safety during travel.
